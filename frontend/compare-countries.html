Kazakhstan vs Germany
Kazakhstan emits ~13.5 t CO₂/capita vs Germany's 8.1 t. Despite Germany's larger economy, its aggressive Energiewende policy cut emissions by 40% since 1990. Kazakhstan has only achieved 16%.
☀️
Renewables Gap
Germany reached 59% renewables share in 2023. UAE is rapidly scaling solar with 15% RE despite being an oil state. Kazakhstan sits at ~15% — mostly old hydro, with wind and solar under 5%.
📈
Regional Context
Uzbekistan has similar intensity but lower per-capita emissions due to less industry. Mongolia is more coal-dependent (90%+). Kazakhstan has the strongest institutional capacity in the region to lead transition.
